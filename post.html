September 30, 2021 The impact of Blockchain technology to Financial Audit
Osca Patric September 30

Audit has evolved through the years from ancient checking of activities in the ancient civilizations of China, Egypt and Greece to the advent of the Industrial revolution in the mid nineteenth century in the UK.The traditional conformance role of auditing shifted from ensuring the credibility and integrity of financial information to not only enhancing the credibility of the financial statement but also provide value-added services such as reporting on irregularities ,identifying business risks and advising management on the internal control environment.

Click me & Read More!

We are now in the middle of a technological revolution and block chain will create a huge disruption in the audit field, auditors will be required to embrace change in order to meet the needs of the business environment. For instance, auditors will be required to audit crypto assets and other emerging digital finance assets, which were nonexistent at some point in time. Block chain is a technology that allows users in a transaction to access the same data instantaneously. It is a peer-peer, internet-based ledger which includes all transactions since its creation. All users maintain an identical copy of the ledger of every transaction. Many have argued that block chain would displace the auditor’s role since all transactions are captured in an immutable block chain. The principal objective of an audit is to provide reasonable assurance that all the transactions are supported by evidence that is reliable, relevant, objective, verifiable, and accurate. Even though the transactions are captured in a fixed block chain, the transactions may still be fraudulent, unauthorized, or incorrectly classified in the financial statements. The technological revolution is seeing more industries migrating into block chain solutions. This is a welcome development for auditors, as it will easen their work by eliminating manual data extraction and tedious audit planning procedures. The audit process will be hastened as supporting documents could be encrypted and linked to a block chain. Human error may also be reduced as access to information will be consistent and readily available. The auditor’s skill will be spent better at considering high-level questions, technology, advisory, and giving transformative solutions to clients.

September 29, 20121 ADVICE FOR CHINESE BUSINESSES OPERATING IN KENYA
Eunice Ago September 2021

What strategies can Chinese investors use to reform their image and balance in Kenya? It’s been almost two decades after Kenya opened her arms to Chinese trade partnerships. The relationship has thrived over the years and has seen major Chinese companies and developments take root in all parts of Kenya. Job opportunities for the locals have been created through several partnerships in exchange of the quality infrastructure by Chinese companies across the country. However, it is paramount to accept the challenges that the partnership has faced so far and the mixed reactions that have been enacted by the locals over the years. Having lived in both countries, experiencing and understanding their social, political, and economical cultures, I have identified the strengths and weaknesses of both Chinese and Kenyan ways of business operation and have found ways in which the two communities can co-exist harmoniously and thus bridge the gap divide and come up with a win-win business situation..

Click me & Read More!

To be able to further elaborate on how to go about this, I will highlight some of the practices that were practiced by our Indian brothers who came to Kenya before the Chinese. They have managed to invest in Kenya since Independence. Kenyans have in many years worked harmoniously with the Indian community. It is possible that Chinese investors can borrow some ideas on how other foreigners have been able to co-exist with the locals. Speaking from the angle of a Chinese expert and business consultant, one of the major challenges we have witnessed is the Chinese understanding of the local tax code. There has been a sharp increase in tax risks with Chinese immigrants and the Kenya revenue authority. This has negatively affected both the famous and upcoming Chinese companies as many experience losses through heavy fines from the government. Companies and individuals should take the initiative to understand the KRA tax laws and comply with them. Through my interaction with Chinese businessmen, it is important to know that most of them are not ignorant of the laws but it is simply negligence to act promptly in obedience to the laws. Many have risked by assuming that they can always get away by giving bribes. However, the expensive assumption that they can always buy their way out of any offense has caused more harm than good. Kenyans need not take the law into their own hands by accepting bribes either but rather seize such mentality by handling the Chinese investors in a fair manner according to the law. The Indian community for example has thrived in the business and has been embraced by Kenyans fully. This is not in absence of differences that had to be confirmed as time went by. However, the major glue to binding the two communities has been that both speak the language of compassion and charity. Many charity foundations targeted to help /empower the locals have been a point of unity. Apart from employment, I recommend that the Chinese community in Kenya also add value to the locals through acts of charity like offering scholarships or donations to the needy . Empowering Kenyans in any way will unite the communities together.
